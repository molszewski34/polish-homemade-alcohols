Rugosa rose wine

When you hear about rugosa rose you probably never saw any of their fruits, not to mention tasting a wine made of it. That is fine. Many of homemade wines are experimental and uses fruits which grows nearby and it the same case with this one. This year i tried to brew some special wine and effect is somehow strange but quiete good.

Rugosa rose is not typical fruit for wine. When you read wikipedia about this plant you get information about usage in chinesse desserts and flower jams. As it is rugosa rose is very fine to make it and i saw people gathering her fruits just to have something special which you cannot find in markets. I would personally never bothered to make even single jar of it. Why? Because one jams are made for sandwitches, some for cakes and some are so aromatic you can't eat them in big portions. If i would use some rugosa rose jam i would probably use it for polish donuts as filling or add gelatine and make a cookies with filling on top, but as i make donuts once per year and cookies are also not something i eat every month i decided to make some wine of it. As you read further you will probably start to understand my decision.

To be honest. I would probably never found this wine if didn't live near the sea. Rugosa roses are invasive species and grows without any complication near roads, beaches and meadows. I don't know how much of it grow in other regions of poland but here you can find it almost everywhere. So when you have so many fruits why don't use it for wine.

Picking fruits

Fruits are ready to pick near september or even october. Ideal moment is when they are between being tender and soft. They must be tender to pick them and soft to pour more juice during fermentation. I always want more juice. With juice comes more flavor. Harvesting is easier as gathering wild rose (Rosa canina) but still hard enough to be annoying. Every branch has countless thorns so the only good way for me is pick them touching lower part of fruit and pull. Sometimes fruit likes to lose small portion of lower part. Second annoying thing is consistence of fruit. They are like wet flower compacted in mass and have small fibers. That is why next time i gonna use some gloves for protection. After 45 min, me and my brother gathered 5 kg which is not good, not terrible.

Strainer is full of small particles and need to be clean and sanitize after every filtration.

Preparing fruits

There are 2 ways of preparing. One way is slow and used in making jams and second is used to prepare wine. The best way to clean fruits is by using water. Take a bucket with lid or any hermetic container, pour water, add fruits, close the lid and then shake it until most of seeds are out. Then take a sieve and gather all the seads from surface. You can also skip this step if you want your wine a bit bitter.

Preparing a batch

10l of wine

3kg of prepared rugosa rose fruits (5 kg of unprepared)

9l of water

2.6kg of sugar

3g wine nutrient

saccharomyces cerevisiae yeasts 16%

Tools: rubber hose, blg meter, strainer - bigger the better, second bucket min. 12l

Process is very simple. Put fruits in jar, pour hot water with mixed sugar. Water must be boiled before you add. It will prevent bacterial infection and mxing sugar is easier in hot water. Prepare yeasts. Yeasts must be added below 30 deg or they gonna die. When you finish adding all ingridients you can add 3g of nutrients. After that batch must be check with Blg meter. It is simple device to check level of sugar in water. Batch should have around 30-32 blg and ferment to 16% alcohol.

Fermentation is quick a last around 1 to max 2 weeks. When Blg is 0 move fruits out of batch. Then wait around 1-2 hours for batch to calm down. During this time most of "orange particles" move down and you can start first filtration. This wine need to be filter atleast few times and during first filtration you will need to use a strainer and ruber hose. Most of leftovers should be at the bottom of container but in this wine particles are in whole wine so be prepared and filter from the start. There is so many particles in upper level you will need to use measuring cup. I used 500 ml cup. Just take sanitized cup and pour it into strainer until bucket is empty. It can take atleast half hour. After that you can add more sugar for flavor.

Batch before filtration had a lot of dust.

Whole maturing last alteast 1 year and batch should be filter atleast once per 3 months. With every filtration should be easier and quicker.
